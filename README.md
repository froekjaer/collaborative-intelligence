# Collaborative Intelligence

Collaborative Intelligence is an open research initiative exploring whether people, artificial intelligence and governed systems can improve understanding together in ways that are transparent, accountable and reproducible.

This repository provides the broader research context for related projects, publications, cases and experiments.

## Current project

[Mission Framework](https://github.com/froekjaer/mission-framework) is the first formal framework developed within this research programme.

Mission Framework explores how governed missions can create justified, evidence-grounded change in reality. Its v0.2 Review Candidate is being prepared for independent academic, practitioner and AI-assisted review.

## Research posture

Collaborative Intelligence is treated as a working proposition rather than an established discipline.

The programme seeks to determine:

- whether the concept contributes useful questions, methods or structures
- where it overlaps existing research and practice
- what evidence would support or falsify its propositions
- which governance mechanisms are necessary for responsible collaboration
- where human–AI collaboration improves or weakens understanding and decisions

Participation in this programme should not be interpreted as endorsement of its projects, contributors or conclusions.

## Principles

- Reality has priority over models.
- Evidence has priority over authority.
- Uncertainty and contradiction should remain visible.
- Consequential decisions require identifiable accountability.
- AI output is analytical input, not authority by declaration.
- Concepts and methods should remain replaceable when better alternatives emerge.

## Invitation

Researchers, practitioners, engineers, governance specialists and other critical readers are invited to examine the work, identify overlap, challenge assumptions and propose better alternatives.

The objective is not to preserve the original ideas. It is to improve understanding.
