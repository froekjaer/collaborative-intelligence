# Collaborative Intelligence

**Foundation Release v1.0**

Collaborative Intelligence is an open research programme examining how people, artificial intelligence and governed systems may improve understanding and action together in ways that remain transparent, accountable, contestable and reproducible.

This repository is the programme-level entry point. It provides the shared vision, research architecture and governance context for the repositories in which semantic theory, reference missions and publication mechanisms are developed.

## Research vision

The programme investigates a central question:

> Under what conditions can human and artificial intelligence collaborate to create better justified knowledge, decisions and outcomes without obscuring uncertainty, provenance or accountability?

Collaborative Intelligence is treated as a research proposition rather than an established discipline. Its concepts are expected to change when evidence, comparison with prior work or practical application demonstrates a better formulation.

## Programme architecture

The research programme separates four concerns so that each can evolve and be evaluated without confusing theory, application and communication:

```text
Collaborative Intelligence
        │
        ├── Mission Framework
        │     Semantic core and normative concepts
        │
        ├── Mission Solar Eclipse
        │     First reference implementation and empirical challenge
        │
        └── Publication Pipeline
              Reproducible transformation of source material into publications
```

### [Mission Framework](https://github.com/froekjaer/mission-framework)

Mission Framework is the semantic core of the programme. It defines the canonical vocabulary, Mission Loop, evidence principles, governance semantics and architectural propositions used to describe how governed missions create justified, evidence-grounded change in reality.

Normative meaning belongs in Mission Framework. Implementations and publications may express or test that meaning, but should not silently redefine it.

### [Mission Solar Eclipse](https://github.com/froekjaer/mission-solar-eclipse)

Mission Solar Eclipse is the first reference implementation. It applies the framework to a real, time-bound and observable mission and records where the semantic model is useful, ambiguous, incomplete or contradicted by practice.

The reference mission is therefore both an implementation and a research instrument. Findings that concern the framework itself are returned through the Framework Findings process rather than resolved locally by changing canonical terms.

### [Publication Pipeline](https://github.com/froekjaer/-Publication-Pipeline)

Publication Pipeline defines how reviewed Markdown sources can be transformed reproducibly into books, articles, PDF documents, GitHub Pages and presentations. It separates canonical source material from generated publication artefacts and makes editorial transformations visible and repeatable.

## Research cycle

The programme follows a deliberately iterative cycle:

```text
Semantic proposition
        ↓
Reference implementation
        ↓
Observation and evidence
        ↓
Framework finding
        ↓
Review and disposition
        ↓
Revised semantics or documented rejection
        ↓
Publication and external challenge
```

No repository is authoritative for every concern. Programme coherence depends on explicit links and disciplined feedback between repositories.

## Research posture

The programme seeks to determine:

- whether its concepts contribute useful questions, methods or structures
- where they overlap, extend or conflict with existing research and professional practice
- what observations would support, qualify or falsify its propositions
- which governance mechanisms are necessary for responsible human–AI collaboration
- where artificial intelligence improves analysis and where it introduces distortion, opacity or misplaced authority
- whether the programme can produce results that are independently understandable and reproducible

Participation in this programme should not be interpreted as endorsement of its projects, contributors or conclusions.

## Shared principles

- Reality has priority over models.
- Evidence has priority over authority.
- Uncertainty and contradiction should remain visible.
- Consequential decisions require identifiable accountability.
- AI output is analytical input, not authority by declaration.
- Normative semantics and implementation-specific choices must remain distinguishable.
- Concepts, methods and tools should remain replaceable when better alternatives emerge.
- Publication should preserve traceability to reviewed source material.

## Foundation Release v1.0

Foundation Release v1.0 establishes the common research architecture across the four repositories. It does not claim theoretical completeness or empirical validation. It defines a coherent starting point from which semantic development, reference implementation, findings, review and publication can proceed as one research programme.

## Invitation

Researchers, practitioners, engineers, governance specialists and other critical readers are invited to examine the work, identify overlap, challenge assumptions and propose better alternatives.

The objective is not to preserve the original ideas. It is to improve understanding.
